Rule 34(b), Uniform Rules of the Alaska State Legislature, is amended to read: 05 (b) Abstention. Except as provided in this subsection, every [EVERY] 06 member present in the house shall vote. A member may abstain from voting if 07 [UNLESS] the house, for special reasons, permits a member to abstain. A member 08 shall request to abstain from voting if the member reasonably believes that the 09 member's vote on a question would constitute a violation of AS 24.60 (Legislative 10 Ethics Act). All motions excusing a member from voting shall be made before the 11 house divides or before the call for the yeas and nays is commenced. A member 12 requesting to be excused from voting shall [MAY] make a brief oral statement of the 13 reasons for the request, including, if applicable, specific provisions of AS 24.60 that 14 support the member's request to abstain. The [AND THE] question of granting 15 permission to abstain shall be taken without further debate. A member may not 16 explain a vote, may not discuss the question while the yeas and nays are being called, 01 and may not change a vote after the roll call result has been announced. Upon division 02 and count of the house of any question, a member who is not within the chamber may 03 not be counted. A member may not be permitted to abstain except upon a majority 04 vote [THE UNANIMOUS CONSENT] of the membership.
