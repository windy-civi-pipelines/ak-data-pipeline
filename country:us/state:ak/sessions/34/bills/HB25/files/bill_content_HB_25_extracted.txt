AS 17.20 is amended by adding a new section to read: 04 Sec. 17.20.035. Disposable food service ware. (a) Except as provided in (e) 05 and (f) of this section, a restaurant may not provide prepared food in polystyrene foam 06 disposable food service ware. 07 (b) The state may not use or purchase polystyrene foam disposable food 08 service ware. 09 (c) A restaurant that uses disposable food service ware shall use biodegradable 10 or compostable disposable food service ware, unless the restaurant has received an 11 exemption under (f) of this section. 12 (d) The department shall recommend that a restaurant reuse food service ware 13 in place of disposable food service ware. 14 (e) The restriction on use of polystyrene foam disposable food service ware 15 under (a) of this section does not apply to 01 (1) prepared food prepared or packaged outside the state; 02 (2) polystyrene foam coolers and ice chests intended for reuse; 03 (3) an area affected by a disaster emergency for the duration of the 04 disaster emergency. 05 (f) A restaurant may apply to the department for an exemption from the 06 requirements of this section. The department may grant an exemption to the restaurant 07 for a particular product or type of disposable food service ware if the restaurant 08 demonstrates to the department that 09 (1) an affordable biodegradable or compostable product is not 10 available to replace a polystyrene foam product; or 11 (2) applying the requirements of this section to the particular product 12 or type of disposable food service ware would cause undue hardship to the restaurant. 13 (g) In this section, 14 (1) "biodegradable or compostable" means that all of the elements 15 completely break down and return to nature or decompose into elements found in 16 nature within a reasonably short period after customary disposal; 17 (2) "disaster emergency" has the meaning given in AS 26.23.900; 18 (3) "disposable food service ware" means a container, bowl, plate, tray, 19 carton, cup, lid, or other item designated for one-time use in which a restaurant or 20 customer places or packages prepared food so that the food may be taken from the 21 restaurant for consumption; 22 (4) "polystyrene foam" means Styrofoam, blown polystyrene, or an 23 expanded foam that is made of thermoplastic petrochemical materials using a styrene 24 monomer and processed by fusion of polymer spheres, injection molding, foam 25 molding, extrusion-blow molding, or other techniques; 26 (5) "restaurant" includes a place maintained for the sale and on- 27 premise consumption of food and a street vendor who sells food prepared for 28 immediate consumption. 29 * Sec. 2.
