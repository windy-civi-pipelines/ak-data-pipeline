AS 17.20 is amended by adding a new section to read: 05 Sec. 17.20.035. Disposable food service ware. (a) Except as provided in (d) - 06 (f) of this section, a restaurant may not provide prepared food in polystyrene foam 07 disposable food service ware. 08 (b) The state may not use or purchase polystyrene foam disposable food 09 service ware. 10 (c) The department shall recommend that a restaurant reuse food service ware 11 in place of disposable food service ware. 12 (d) The department may adopt regulations that allow a restaurant to provide 13 prepared food in a clean container provided by a customer of the restaurant. The 14 regulations must establish procedures to prevent cross-contamination. 01 (e) The restriction on use of polystyrene foam disposable food service ware 02 under (a) of this section does not apply to 03 (1) prepared food prepared or packaged outside the state; 04 (2) polystyrene foam coolers and ice chests intended for reuse; 05 (3) an area affected by a disaster emergency for the duration of the 06 disaster emergency. 07 (f) A restaurant may apply to the department for an exemption from the 08 requirements of this section. The department may grant an exemption to the restaurant 09 for a particular product or type of disposable food service ware if the restaurant 10 demonstrates to the department that 11 (1) an affordable product is not available to replace a polystyrene foam 12 product; or 13 (2) applying the requirements of this section to the particular product 14 or type of disposable food service ware would cause undue financial and logistical 15 hardship to the restaurant. 16 (g) In this section, 17 (1) "disaster emergency" has the meaning given in AS 26.23.900; 18 (2) "disposable food service ware" means a container, bowl, plate, tray, 19 carton, cup, lid, or other item designated for one-time use in which a restaurant or 20 customer places or packages prepared food so that the food may be taken from the 21 restaurant for consumption; 22 (3) "polystyrene foam" means Styrofoam, blown polystyrene, or an 23 expanded foam that is made of thermoplastic petrochemical materials using a styrene 24 monomer and processed by fusion of polymer spheres, injection molding, foam 25 molding, extrusion-blow molding, or other techniques; 26 (4) "restaurant" includes a place maintained for the sale and on- 27 premises consumption of food and a street vendor who sells food prepared for 28 immediate consumption. 29 * Sec. 2. The uncodified law of the State of Alaska is amended by adding a new section to 30 read: 31 TRANSITION: POLYSTYRENE PRODUCTS. (a) Notwithstanding AS 17.20.035, 01 enacted by sec. 1 of this Act, a restaurant may provide prepared food in polystyrene foam 02 disposable food service ware after the effective date of this Act if the restaurant obtained the 03 polystyrene foam disposable food service ware before the effective date of this Act. 04 (b) Notwithstanding AS 17.20.035, enacted by sec. 1 of this Act, the state may use 05 polystyrene foam disposable food service ware after the effective date of this Act if the state 06 purchased the polystyrene foam disposable food service ware before the effective date of this 07 Act. 08 * Sec. 3.
