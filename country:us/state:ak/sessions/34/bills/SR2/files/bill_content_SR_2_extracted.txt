and 04 WHEREAS legislation enacted by the federal government has far-reaching effects on 05 the economy of the state; and 06 WHEREAS there is renewed interest in the United States Congress in exploring the 07 ramifications of federal legislation on the states and their economies; and 08 WHEREAS the legislature should take an active role in exploring opportunities to 09 promote world trade and assist the United States Congress in understanding the effect of 10 federal legislation on the state's economy; 11 BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate that a Senate Special Committee on World Trade 12 is established to study issues relating to world trade; and be it 13 FURTHER RESOLVED that the President of the Senate shall determine the number 14 of senators to serve on the committee, appoint the members to the committee, and designate 15 members to co-chair the committee; and be it 16 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Senate Special Committee on World Trade may 01 meet during and between sessions of the Thirty-Fourth Alaska State Legislature and is 02 terminated on the convening of the First Regular Session of the Thirty-Fifth Alaska State 03 Legislature.
