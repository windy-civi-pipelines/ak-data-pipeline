AS 29.10.200(56) is amended to read: 05 (56) AS 29.45.650(c), (d), (e), (f), (i), (j), (k), [AND] (l), and (m) 06 (sales and use tax); 07 * Sec. 2. AS 29.10.200(57) is amended to read: 08 (57) AS 29.45.700(d), (e), (g), [AND] (h), and (i) (sales and use tax); 09 * Sec. 3. AS 29.45.650(a) is amended to read: 10 (a) Except as provided in AS 04.21.010(c), AS 29.45.750, and in (f), (h), (i), 11 (j), (k), [AND] (l), and (m) of this section, a borough may levy and collect a sales tax 12 on sales, rents, and services provided in the borough. The sales tax may apply to any 13 or all of these sources. Notwithstanding other statutes, exemptions may be granted by 14 ordinance. A borough may wholly or partially exempt a source from a borough sales 01 tax that is taxed by a city in that borough under AS 29.45.700. 02 * Sec. 4. AS 29.45.650 is amended by adding a new subsection to read: 03 (m) A borough may not levy or collect a sales or use tax on the sale or 04 exchange of specie. This subsection applies to home rule and general law 05 municipalities. In this subsection, "specie" has the meaning given in AS 44.12.400(d). 06 * Sec. 5. AS 29.45.700(a) is amended to read: 07 (a) A city in a borough that levies and collects areawide sales and use taxes 08 may levy sales and use taxes on all sources taxed by the borough in the manner 09 provided for boroughs. Except as provided in (d), (e), (g), [AND] (h), and (i) of this 10 section, the assembly may by ordinance authorize a city to levy and collect sales and 11 use taxes on other sources. 12 * Sec. 6. AS 29.45.700 is amended by adding a new subsection to read: 13 (i) A city may not levy or collect a sales or use tax on the sale or exchange of 14 specie. This subsection applies to home rule and general law municipalities. In this 15 subsection, "specie" has the meaning given in AS 44.12.400(d). 16 * Sec. 7. AS 44.12 is amended by adding a new section to read: 17 Article 5. Legal Tender. 18 Sec. 44.12.400. Legal tender. (a) To the fullest extent allowed by art. I, sec. 19 10, clause 1, of the Constitution of the United States, legal tender in the state includes 20 specie issued by 21 (1) the federal government at any time if the federal government 22 considers the specie legal tender; 23 (2) a foreign government if, at the time of a sale or exchange, the 24 foreign government considers the specie legal tender and the foreign government is 25 (A) recognized by the United States Department of State as 26 being the legitimate government of a country whose existence is recognized by 27 the United States and has not been designated as a foreign adversary under 28 federal law or regulation; 29 (B) a subnational unit of a government described in (A) of this 30 paragraph that is authorized by the laws of that government to issue its own 31 currency; or 01 (C) an international currency union made up exclusively of 02 countries described in (A) of this paragraph; or 03 (3) the state government if 04 (A) a court of competent jurisdiction, by final and unappealable 05 order, ruled the specie to be within state authority to designate as legal tender; 06 and 07 (B) the state government considers the specie legal tender. 08 (b) A person is not required to accept gold or silver specie as tender. 09 (c) The Legislative Budget and Audit Committee shall study the possibility of 10 establishing additional forms of legal tender for the payment of debts, including public 11 charges, taxes, and other money owed to the state. If the committee determines that 12 additional forms of tender are practical and beneficial, the committee shall prepare 13 legislation establishing an additional form of legal tender for introduction. 14 (d) In this section, 15 (1) "legal tender" means a recognized medium of exchange for the 16 payment of debts; 17 (2) "specie" means gold or silver valued primarily based on its metal 18 content and in the form of 19 (A) coin; or 20 (B) bullion that is stamped or imprinted with its weight and 21 purity.
