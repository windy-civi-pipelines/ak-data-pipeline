HB 124: "An Act relating to the Alaska Industrial Development and Export Authority; and providing for an effective date." 

00                            HOUSE BILL NO. 124                                                                          
01 "An Act relating to the Alaska Industrial Development and Export Authority; and                                         
02 providing for an effective date."                                                                                       
03 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF ALASKA:                                                                
04    * Section 1. The uncodified law of the State of Alaska is amended by adding a new section                          
05 to read:                                                                                                                
06       SHORT TITLE. This Act may be known as the AIDEA Accountability Act.                                             
07    * Sec. 2. AS 44.88.030(a) is amended to read:                                                                      
08            (a)  The membership of the authority consists of                                                             
09                 (1)  the commissioner of revenue and the commissioner of commerce,                                      
10       community, and economic development; [AND]                                                                        
11 (2)  four [FIVE] public members appointed by the governor and                                                   
12 confirmed by a majority of the members of the legislature in joint session, each of                                 
13 whom has expertise in private sector business or industry, or both, and possesses                                       
14       demonstrated leadership skills;                                                                               



01                 (3)  one public member, appointed by the governor and confirmed                                     
02       by a majority of the members of the legislature in joint session, who represents                              
03       an environmental advocacy organization;                                                                       
04                 (4)  one public member, appointed by the president of the senate,                                   
05       who is a former legislator or meets the qualifications in (2) or (3) of this                                  
06       subsection; and                                                                                               
07                 (5)  one public member, appointed by the speaker of the house of                                    
08       representatives, who is a former legislator or meets the qualifications in (2) or (3)                         
09       of this subsection.                                                                                           
10    * Sec. 3. AS 44.88.030(c) is amended to read:                                                                      
11 (c)  Public members of the authority described in (a)(2) - (5) [(a)(2)] of this                                     
12 section serve staggered three-year [AT THE PLEASURE OF THE GOVERNOR                                                 
13       FOR TWO-YEAR] terms.                                                                                              
14    * Sec. 4. AS 44.88.050(c) is amended to read:                                                                      
15 (c)  The authority may appoint persons as officers it considers advisable,                                              
16 including an executive director, and may employ professional advisors, counsel,                                         
17 technical experts, agents, and other employees it considers advisable. The executive                                    
18 director and employees of the authority are in the exempt service under AS 39.25. The                               
19 authority shall adopt regulations or bylaws establishing a personnel policy,                                        
20       including minimum qualifications for the executive director.                                                  
21    * Sec. 5. AS 44.88.080(1) is amended to read:                                                                      
22                 (1)  subject to AS 44.88.225, to sue and be sued;                                                   
23    * Sec. 6. AS 44.88.085(b) is amended to read:                                                                      
24 (b)  The authority may adopt regulations [UNDER THIS SECTION BY                                                         
25 MOTION OR] by resolution [OR IN ANY OTHER MANNER PERMITTED BY ITS                                                       
26       BYLAWS].                                                                                                          
27    * Sec. 7. AS 44.88.085(d) is amended to read:                                                                      
28 (d)  Except as provided in (e) of this section, at least 30 [15] days before the                                    
29 adoption, amendment, or repeal of a regulation, the authority shall give public notice                                  
30 of the proposed action by publishing the notice in at least three newspapers of general                                 
31 circulation in the state and by mailing a copy of the notice to every person who has                                    



01 filed a request for notice of proposed regulations with the authority. The public notice                                
02 must include a statement of the time, place, and nature of the proceedings for the                                      
03 adoption, amendment, or repeal of the regulation and must include an informative                                        
04 summary of the subject of the proposed action. On the date and at the time and place                                    
05 designated in the notice, the authority shall give each interested person or an                                         
06 authorized representative of the person, or both, the opportunity to present statements,                                
07 arguments, or contentions orally or in writing and shall give each member                                           
08 [MEMBERS] of the public an opportunity to present oral statements, arguments, or                                        
09 contentions for not less than two minutes [A TOTAL PERIOD OF AT LEAST ONE                                           
10 HOUR]. The authority shall consider all relevant matter presented to it before taking                                   
11 the proposed action on the regulation, and shall publish written responses to public                                
12 comments. At a hearing under this subsection, the authority may continue or postpone                                
13 the hearing to a time and place determined by the authority and announced at the                                        
14 hearing before taking the action to continue or postpone the hearing. The authority                                 
15 shall publish written justification when adopting, amending, or repealing a                                         
16 regulation. A regulation adopted, amended, or repealed by the authority may vary                                    
17 from the informative summary specified in this subsection if the subject matter of the                                  
18 action taken on the regulation remains the same and if the original notice of the                                       
19 proposed action was written so as to assure that members of the public are reasonably                                   
20 notified of the subject matter of the proposed action in order for them to determine                                    
21 whether their interests could be affected by the authority's proposed action on that                                    
22       subject.                                                                                                          
23    * Sec. 8. AS 44.88 is amended by adding a new section to read:                                                     
24 Sec. 44.88.107. Limit on value of funds and accounts. The aggregate value                                             
25 of all funds and accounts in the authority may not exceed $500,000,000. On June 30 of                                   
26 each fiscal year, the amount of unexpended and unobligated money required to reduce                                     
27 the aggregate value of all funds and accounts in the authority to $500,000,000 lapses                                   
28 into the general fund. Money that lapses under this section is not payment of a                                         
29       dividend under AS 44.88.088.                                                                                      
30    * Sec. 9. AS 44.88 is amended by adding a new section to read:                                                     
31 Sec. 44.88.179. Project oversight. Before agreeing to expend more than                                                



01       $10,000,000 on a project, the authority shall                                                                     
02 (1)  unless the project is to be located in the unorganized borough,                                                    
03 obtain a certified copy of a resolution of the governing body of the political                                          
04 subdivision of the state, if any, in which the project is to be located, consenting to the                              
05 location of the project; the consent need only refer to the general nature of the project;                              
06                 (2)  find, based on all information reasonably available to it, that                                    
07                      (A)  the project and its development under this chapter will be                                    
08            economically advantageous to the state and the general public welfare and will                               
09            contribute to the economic growth of the state;                                                              
10                      (B)  the project applicant, if any, is financially responsible;                                    
11 (C)  provisions to meet increased demand on public facilities                                                           
12            that might result from the project are reasonably assured;                                                   
13 (D)  the project will provide or retain employment reasonably                                                           
14 related to the amount of the financing by the authority, if any, considering the                                        
15 amount of investment for each employee for comparable facilities and other                                              
16            relevant factors;                                                                                            
17 (E)  the project is economically and financially feasible and able                                                      
18 to produce revenue adequate to repay the bonds or loans, if any, with which it                                          
19            is financed; and                                                                                             
20                      (F)  the project complies with applicable law;                                                     
21 (3)  compile and make available to the public a document that                                                           
22 summarizes the projected economic, social, and environmental effects of the project;                                    
23 and, in conjunction with the Department of Fish and Game, the Department of Natural                                     
24 Resources, the Department of Environmental Conservation, and the Department of                                          
25 Labor and Workforce Development, the authority shall conduct a public hearing on                                        
26       the projected effects of the project;                                                                             
27 (4)  submit a report that includes the documents and findings required                                                  
28 under (1) - (3) of this section to the senate secretary and the chief clerk of the house of                             
29       representatives and notify the legislature that the report is available; and                                      
30                 (5)  obtain legislative approval by law.                                                                
31    * Sec. 10. AS 44.88.180(a) is amended to read:                                                                     



01 (a)  A member of the authority may not vote on a resolution of the authority                                            
02 relating to a lease or contract to be entered into by the authority under this chapter if                               
03 the member is a party to the lease or contract or has a direct or indirect ownership or                             
04 equity interest in a firm, partnership, corporation, or association that may be a party to                              
05 the contract or lease. A resolution of the authority that is approved by a majority of the                              
06 members who are not barred from voting under this subsection is a valid action of the                                   
07       authority for all purposes.                                                                                       
08    * Sec. 11. AS 44.88.205(b) is amended to read:                                                                     
09 (b)  To further ensure effective budgetary decision making by the legislature,                                          
10       the authority shall                                                                                               
11 (1)  annually review the authority's assets to determine whether assets                                                 
12 of the authority exceed an amount required to fulfill the purposes of the authority as                                  
13 defined in this chapter; in making its review, the authority shall determine whether,                                   
14 and to what extent, assets in excess of the amount required to fulfill the purposes of                                  
15       the authority during at least the next fiscal year are available without                                          
16                      (A)  breaching any agreement entered into by the authority;                                        
17 (B)  materially impairing the operations or financial integrity of                                                      
18            the authority; or                                                                                            
19 (C)  materially affecting the ability of the authority to fulfill the                                                   
20            authority's purposes set out in AS 44.88.070; and                                                            
21 (2)  by January 10 of each year, make available to the legislature [BY                                              
22 JANUARY 10 OF EACH YEAR] a complete accounting of all assets of the authority                                           
23 and a report of the review and determination made under (1) of this subsection,                                     
24 submit the accounting and report to the senate secretary and the chief clerk of                                     
25 the house of representatives, and notify the legislature that the accounting and                                    
26 report are available; the accounting shall be audited by the auditor who conducts the                               
27 audit required by AS 44.88.200 and must include a full description of all loan interest                                 
28       and principal payments and program receipts, including                                                            
29 (A)  loan commitment fees received by or accrued to the                                                                 
30            authority during the preceding fiscal year; and                                                              
31 (B)  all income earned on assets of the authority during that                                                           



01            period.                                                                                                      
02    * Sec. 12. AS 44.88.210(a) is amended to read:                                                                     
03 (a)  By January 10 of each year, the authority shall publish a report for                                               
04 distribution to the governor, the legislature, and the public, submit the report to the                         
05 senate secretary and the chief clerk of the house of representatives, and [. THE                                    
06 AUTHORITY SHALL] notify the legislature that the report is available. The                                           
07 authority shall develop performance metrics to evaluate, and the report must                                        
08 include analysis of, the effect of the authority's activities on job creation,                                      
09 supported industry growth, financial and technical assistance provided to state                                     
10 businesses, and private investment increases. The report shall be written in easily                                 
11 understandable language. The report must include a financial statement audited by an                                    
12 independent outside auditor, the findings from the authority's annual audit, a                                      
13 statement of the authority's investments under this chapter, including an appraisal of                              
14 the investments at market value, a comparison of the authority's performance with the                                   
15 goals of the authority and the levels of bonding and investment activities anticipated in                               
16 the previous year's report under (b) of this section, a list of all actions taken at each                           
17 meeting of the authority since the previous year's annual report, and any other                                     
18 information the members of the authority believe would be of interest to the governor,                                  
19 the legislature, and the public. The annual income statement and balance sheet of the                                   
20 authority shall be published in at least one newspaper in each judicial district. The                                   
21       authority may also publish other reports it considers desirable to carry out its purpose.                         
22    * Sec. 13. AS 44.88.215 is repealed and reenacted to read:                                                         
23 Sec. 44.88.215. Records and information; public records. (a) The records or                                           
24 information submitted to the authority are public records under AS 40.25.110 -                                          
25       40.25.220.                                                                                                        
26 (b)  Before accepting submission of records or information, the authority shall                                         
27 require a written waiver acknowledging that the submission is a public record under                                     
28       AS 40.25.110 - 40.25.220.                                                                                         
29    * Sec. 14. AS 44.88 is amended by adding a new section to read:                                                    
30 Sec. 44.88.225. Attorney general lawsuit approval. (a) The authority shall                                            
31       obtain approval in writing from the attorney general before filing a lawsuit.                                     



01 (b)  If the attorney general approves a lawsuit, the attorney general shall notify                                      
02 the senate secretary and the chief clerk of the house of representatives of the approval                                
03 in writing. The notification must describe the lawsuit and explain the reasons for the                                  
04       approval.                                                                                                         
05            (c)  This section does not require approval from the attorney general for the                                
06       authority to respond to a lawsuit filed against the authority.                                                    
07    * Sec. 15. AS 44.88.380 is repealed.                                                                               
08    * Sec. 16. The uncodified law of the State of Alaska is amended by adding a new section to                         
09 read:                                                                                                                   
10 MEMBERS OF THE ALASKA INDUSTRIAL DEVELOPMENT AND EXPORT                                                                 
11 AUTHORITY: TRANSITION. (a) The terms of the members of the Alaska Industrial                                            
12 Development and Export Authority, other than the commissioner of revenue and the                                        
13 commissioner of commerce, community, and economic development, expire on the date the                                   
14 legislature has confirmed at least two members appointed by the governor under (b) of this                              
15 section.                                                                                                                
16 (b)  Notwithstanding AS 44.88.030(c), as amended by sec. 3 of this Act, members of                                      
17 the Alaska Industrial Development and Export Authority appointed by the governor under                                  
18 AS 44.88.030(a), as amended by sec. 2 of this Act, are appointed by the governor to initial                             
19 terms as follows:                                                                                                       
20            (1)  two members for one year;                                                                               
21            (2)  two members for two years;                                                                            
22            (3)  one member for three years.                                                                             
23 (c)  The president of the senate and the speaker of the house of representatives shall                                  
24 appoint public members of the Alaska Industrial Development and Export Authority under                                  
25 AS 44.88.030(a), as amended by sec. 2 of this Act, on or before the 30th day of the First                               
26 Regular Session of the Thirty-Fifth Alaska State Legislature.                                                           
27 (d)  Nothing in this section prevents the appointment of a person whose term expires                                    
28 under (a) of this section as a member of the Alaska Industrial Development and Export                                   
29 Authority if the person meets the qualifications under AS 44.88.030, as amended by secs. 2                              
30 and 3 of this Act.                                                                                                      
31    * Sec. 17. This Act takes effect January 1, 2027.
