SB 169: "An Act establishing the welcoming Alaska office; establishing the welcoming Alaska center; establishing the Welcoming Alaska Advisory Committee; repealing the office of citizenship assistance; and providing for an effective date." 

00                            SENATE BILL NO. 169                                                                         
01 "An Act establishing the welcoming Alaska office; establishing the welcoming Alaska                                     
02 center; establishing the Welcoming Alaska Advisory Committee; repealing the office of                                   
03 citizenship assistance; and providing for an effective date."                                                           
04 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF ALASKA:                                                                
05    * Section 1. AS 23.05 is amended by adding new sections to read:                                                   
06            Sec. 23.05.131. Welcoming Alaska office. (a) The welcoming Alaska office is                                
07       established in the office of the commissioner. The office shall                                                   
08                 (1)  provide to new immigrants and newcomers employment                                                 
09       information, referrals to employment services, including job placement services, and                              
10       information about how to use labor unions, administrative agencies, and court actions                             
11       to pursue claims or charges of job discrimination, illegal termination of employment,                             
12       sexual harassment, and unsafe working conditions;                                                                 
13                 (2)  consult with stakeholders, including state agencies and local                                      
14       governments, to identify strategies to reduce licensing and other employment barriers                             



01       for new immigrants and newcomers;                                                                                 
02                 (3)  use the education and experience of new immigrants and                                             
03       newcomers to benefit the economy of the state;                                                                    
04                 (4)  contract with other state agencies to develop and administer                                       
05       programs or services that promote entrance into and mobility within the workforce for                             
06       new immigrants and newcomers;                                                                                     
07                 (5)  consult with stakeholders, including state agencies and local                                      
08       governments, to develop and implement a statewide strategy to support businesses in                               
09       recruiting and retaining workers who are new immigrants and newcomers;                                            
10                 (6)  advise the commissioner on                                                                         
11 (A)  improving employment opportunities for new immigrants                                                              
12            and newcomers;                                                                                               
13 (B)  preventing employment discrimination against new                                                                   
14            immigrants and newcomers;                                                                                    
15 (C)  cooperating with state and federal agencies to accomplish                                                          
16            the office's mission;                                                                                        
17 (D)  supporting the economic and social integration of new                                                              
18            immigrants and newcomers; and                                                                                
19 (E)  reducing the exploitation of new immigrants and                                                                    
20            newcomers;                                                                                                   
21 (7)  subject to appropriation, offer grants to support the economic and                                                 
22       social integration of new immigrants and newcomers;                                                               
23 (8)  subject to appropriation, offer grants to state and local government                                               
24 agencies, community-based organizations, and nonprofits that provide individualized                                     
25       support to foreign workers;                                                                                       
26 (9)  serve as a convener for community-based organizations serving                                                      
27       new immigrants and newcomers;                                                                                     
28 (10)  design and implement strategies and programs to make the state a                                                  
29       more welcoming place for new immigrants and newcomers;                                                            
30 (11)  serve as a point of contact for all state agencies regarding issues                                               
31       affecting new immigrants and newcomers;                                                                           



01                 (12)  subject to appropriation, establish the welcoming Alaska center                                   
02 under AS 25.05.134 and operate a toll-free multilingual hotline for referral of services;                               
03 (13)  coordinate with departments and nonprofit organizations to assist                                                 
04 new immigrants and newcomers in improving quality-of-life issues, including access                                      
05 to business opportunities, education, employment, housing, healthcare, and other                                        
06       services;                                                                                                         
07                 (14)  subject to appropriation, assist political subdivisions with fees and                             
08       other costs associated with visa applications, recruitment, and housing support for new                           
09       immigrants and newcomers;                                                                                         
10 (15)  research and analyze economic and demographic trends to better                                                    
11       serve new immigrants and newcomers;                                                                               
12 (16)  coordinate and establish best practices for language access                                                       
13       initiatives for all state agencies;                                                                               
14 (17)  serve as an information clearinghouse on information regarding                                                    
15       new immigrants and newcomers; and                                                                                 
16 (18)  make policy recommendations to the governor and the legislature                                                   
17       on issues affecting new immigrants and newcomers.                                                                 
18 (b)  The office may contract with other organizations as appropriate to further                                         
19       the goals of the office.                                                                                          
20 (c)  The governor shall appoint an individual to serve as the executive director                                        
21 of the office. The executive director is in the partially exempt service under AS 39.25                                 
22       (State Personnel Act).                                                                                            
23 (d)  The department shall adopt regulations necessary to implement this                                                 
24       section.                                                                                                          
25 Sec. 23.05.132. Welcoming Alaska liaison. (a) Each principal department in                                            
26 the executive branch shall designate one staff member to serve as a liaison between                                     
27       the department and the office.                                                                                    
28            (b)  A liaison shall                                                                                         
29 (1)  disseminate information about services offered by the department                                                   
30 that are available to new immigrants and newcomers, department staff, the office, and                                   
31       the public;                                                                                                       



01                 (2)  communicate with the office on issues affecting new immigrants                                     
02       and newcomers;                                                                                                    
03                 (3)  when applicable, coordinate between the office, the liaison's                                      
04       department, and professional boards within the liaison's department to assist the office                          
05       with license applications from new immigrants and newcomers.                                                      
06 Sec. 23.05.133. Welcoming Alaska Advisory Committee. (a) The                                                          
07 Welcoming Alaska Advisory Committee is established in the department and shall be                                       
08 staffed and administered by the office. The advisory committee consists of the                                          
09       following nine members:                                                                                           
10 (1)  one nonvoting member who is a member of the house of                                                               
11       representatives appointed by the speaker of the house of representatives;                                         
12 (2)  one nonvoting member who is a member of the senate appointed by                                                    
13       the president of the senate;                                                                                      
14                 (3)  seven voting members appointed by the governor as follows:                                         
15 (A)  a representative from a nonprofit organization that provides                                                       
16            employment or language services to new immigrants and newcomers;                                             
17                      (B)  a representative from a tribal nonprofit organization;                                        
18 (C)  a representative from the Alaska Council of School                                                                 
19            Administrators;                                                                                              
20                      (D)  a representative from the business community;                                                 
21 (E)  a representative from a nonprofit legal services agency that                                                       
22            provides services to immigrants and refugees;                                                                
23                      (F)  a representative from the criminal justice system;                                            
24                      (G)  a member of the public.                                                                       
25            (b)  The committee shall                                                                                     
26 (1)  assist the office with developing recommendations for policies,                                                    
27 procedures, regulations, and legislation to help ensure state government and state                                      
28 programs are accessible and responsive to the needs of new immigrants and                                               
29       newcomers;                                                                                                        
30 (2)  work with the office to monitor the work of state agencies to                                                      
31       include and retain new immigrants and newcomers in state programs;                                                



01 (3)  conduct public meetings, as requested by the governor or the                                                       
02 office, for the public to comment and make suggestions on attracting to and retaining                                   
03 in the state new immigrants and newcomers and improving the employment                                                  
04 opportunities, inclusion in civic matters, and community connections of new                                             
05       immigrants and newcomers;                                                                                         
06                 (4)  conduct studies, on request of the governor or the office, on issues                               
07       affecting new immigrants and newcomers;                                                                           
08                 (5)  advise the office on ways to increase outreach to new immigrants                                   
09       and newcomers; and                                                                                                
10 (6)  review budget requests of the office and recommend to the                                                          
11       commissioner amounts for appropriations to the office.                                                            
12 (c)  A voting member of the committee appointed by the governor serves at the                                           
13 pleasure of the governor for staggered terms of four years until reappointed or                                         
14 replaced. If a vacancy occurs among the voting members, the governor shall appoint a                                    
15       member for the unexpired portion of the term.                                                                     
16            (d)  Voting members of the committee shall elect a member to serve as chair.                                 
17            (e)  A majority of the committee constitutes a quorum.                                                       
18 (f)  The committee shall meet at least twice a year. A meeting may be held at                                           
19 the call of the chair, by request of a majority of the committee, or at the request of the                              
20       executive director of the office.                                                                                 
21 (g)  A member of the committee receives no salary but is entitled to per diem                                           
22       and travel expenses authorized for boards and commissions under AS 39.20.180.                                     
23 Sec. 23.05.134. Welcoming Alaska center. (a) Subject to appropriation, the                                            
24 office shall establish a welcoming Alaska center in one or more communities to                                          
25 provide virtual and in-person information and assistance for new immigrants and                                         
26 newcomers. The office shall prioritize establishing a welcoming Alaska center in an                                     
27 existing state office facility or on a University of Alaska campus that is easily                                       
28       accessible and conveniently located.                                                                              
29 (b)  A welcoming Alaska center shall provide the following services to new                                              
30       immigrants and newcomers:                                                                                         
31                 (1)  English and Alaska Native language instruction;                                                    



01                 (2)  employment services and training; and                                                              
02                 (3)  assistance with access to government programs and community                                        
03       services.                                                                                                         
04            Sec. 23.05.135. Definitions. In AS 23.05.131 - 23.05.135,                                                  
05                 (1)  "newcomer" means an individual who moved to the state from                                         
06       another state in the preceding 36 months;                                                                         
07                 (2)  "new immigrant" means an individual, or the child of an individual,                                
08       who has arrived, or will arrive, to the state as an immigrant or refugee;                                         
09                 (3)  "office" means the welcoming Alaska office established under                                       
10       AS 23.05.131.                                                                                                     
11    * Sec. 2. AS 39.25.120(c) is amended by adding a new paragraph to read:                                            
12                 (22)  the executive director of the welcoming Alaska office.                                            
13    * Sec. 3. AS 23.05.125 is repealed.                                                                                
14    * Sec. 4. The uncodified law of the State of Alaska is amended by adding a new section to                          
15 read:                                                                                                                   
16 STUDY; REPORT TO THE LEGISLATURE. The welcoming Alaska office                                                           
17 established under AS 23.05.131 shall conduct a study to determine immigration opportunities                             
18 in the state, establish goals for the welcoming Alaska office, and develop performance                                  
19 indicators to measure progress for the welcoming Alaska office. The study must include input                            
20 from employers in the state. The welcoming Alaska office shall prepare a report explaining                              
21 the findings from the study and submit the report by July 1, 2027, to the senate secretary and                          
22 the chief clerk of the house of representatives and notify the legislature that the report is                           
23 available.                                                                                                              
24    * Sec. 5. This Act takes effect July 1, 2026.
