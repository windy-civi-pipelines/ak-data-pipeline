07 and 08 WHEREAS residents of the state believe that the names of the geographic features in 09 the state should be determined by state residents and representatives; and 10 WHEREAS the Athabaskan people and their ancestors have lived in the Interior of 11 the state for more than 10,000 years; and 12 WHEREAS Denali, the traditional Koyukon Athabaskan name, meaning "the high 13 one," was designated as the official name for North America's tallest mountain in 1975 by the 14 state; and 15 WHEREAS, on June 7, 1913, Walter Harper, a 20-year-old man of Athabaskan 01 descent, became the first person to stand on the summit of the mountain; and 02 WHEREAS state lawmakers petitioned the United States Board on Geographic 03 Names to change the mountain's name to "Denali" in 1975, and, in 2015, the United States 04 officially adopted the name Denali for the mountain; and 05 WHEREAS residents of the state from all backgrounds and political persuasions 06 embrace Denali as the rightful name for the tallest mountain in North America; and 07 WHEREAS residents of the state take pride in the fact that people outside the state 08 also recognize Denali as the mountain's well-established name; and 09 WHEREAS both Denali National Park and the mountain are well-known and 10 established landmarks, and changing the mountain's name could cause confusion and 11 negatively affect tourism in the state; and 12 WHEREAS there were once hundreds of languages spoken by Alaska Native peoples, 13 many of which are still used by their descendants today; and 14 WHEREAS the use of, and names and terms from, Athabaskan languages have a rich 15 legacy in the Interior and Canada; and 16 WHEREAS traditional Indigenous names for certain geographic features, places, and 17 communities are commonly used by local, state, and federal government agencies in the state; 18 and 19 WHEREAS residents of the state understand that the use of Indigenous names 20 throughout the state reflects the long history, diverse cultures, traditions, and languages of the 21 state's Alaska Native peoples; and 22 WHEREAS the Alaska State Legislature supports efforts to recognize the heritage of 23 the state's Alaska Native peoples and the continuing importance of the mountain to Alaska 24 Native culture; 25 BE IT RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature respectfully urges the President 26 of the United States, the United States Secretary of the Interior, and the United States Board 27 on Geographic Names to maintain Denali as the name for the tallest mountain in North 28 America and that action to rename the mountain not be taken; and be it 29 FURTHER RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature respectfully urges the 30 members of the Alaska delegation in Congress to advocate on behalf of, and work to preserve, 31 Denali as the name for the tallest mountain in North America. 01 COPIES of this resolution shall be sent to the Honorable Donald J. Trump, President 02 of the United States; the Honorable J. D. Vance, Vice President of the United States and 03 President of the U.S. Senate; the Honorable Walter Cruickshank, Acting United States 04 Secretary of the Interior; the Domestic Names Committee, United States Board on 05 Geographic Names; and the Honorable Lisa Murkowski and the Honorable Dan Sullivan, 06 U.S. Senators, and the Honorable Nick Begich, U.S. Representative, members of the Alaska 07 delegation in Congress.
