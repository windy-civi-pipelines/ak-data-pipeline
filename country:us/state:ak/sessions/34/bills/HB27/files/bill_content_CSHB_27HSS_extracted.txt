AS 18.08.010 is amended to read: 04 Sec. 18.08.010. Administration. The department is responsible for the 05 development, implementation, and maintenance of a statewide comprehensive 06 emergency medical services system and, accordingly, shall 07 (1) coordinate public and private agencies engaged in the planning and 08 delivery of emergency medical services, including trauma care and care for major 09 emergencies, to plan an emergency medical services system; 10 (2) assist public and private agencies to deliver emergency medical 11 services, including trauma care and care for major emergencies, through the award 12 of grants in aid; 13 (3) conduct, encourage, and approve programs of education and 14 training designed to upgrade the knowledge and skills of health personnel involved in 15 emergency medical services, including trauma care and care for major emergencies; 01 (4) establish and maintain a process under which hospitals and clinics 02 could represent themselves to be trauma centers or otherwise capable of treating 03 major emergencies because they voluntarily meet criteria adopted by the department; 04 criteria adopted by the department to implement this paragraph must be based on an 05 applicable national evaluation system. 06 * Sec. 2. AS 18.08.082 is amended by adding a new subsection to read: 07 (d) The commissioner may establish special designations in regulation for 08 varying levels of care for major emergencies provided by a medical facility certified 09 under (a)(3) of this section. The designations shall be based on nationally recognized 10 standards and procedures. 11 * Sec. 3. AS 18.08.200 is amended by adding a new paragraph to read: 12 (15) "major emergency" includes a heart attack or stroke.
